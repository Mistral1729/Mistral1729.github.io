Notes

This is my collection of work in progress lecture notes, which are not direct transcriptions of the lectures but rather draw from various sources (including textbooks and fellow classmates' notes), highly modified to aid my own understanding of the material.

The beautiful $\LaTeX$ template used is entirely based on Adam Kelly's style file.

If you find any errors please contact me at dassayan0013@gmail.com.

Semester 1

  • Real Analysis

    Construction of the reals, supremum and infimum, properties of the reals, point-set topology, Heine-Borel theorem, convergence of sequences and series, Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem, tests for convergence of series.

    4 credits

  • Geometry and Linear Algebra

    Coordinate geometry in 3 dimensions, conicoids, quadric surfaces. Vector spaces, subspaces, linear dependence and independece, basis and span, maximal linearly independent subsets, linear transformations, rank-nullity theorem, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, Cayley-Hamilton theorem.

    4 credits

  • Discrete Mathematics

    Posets, lattice theory, boolean algebra, pigeonhole principle, finite combinatorics, generating functions, graphs and trees, complete graphs, bipartite graphs, connected graphs, Eulerian graphs, Hamiltonian graphs, planar graphs.

    4 credits

  • Probability and Descriptive Statistics

    Random variables, univariate and multivariate probability distributions and densities, expectation, skewness, kurtosis, covariance, linear regression, correlation, Spearman's rank correlation.

    4 credits

  • MATLAB Programming

    An introduction to MATLAB programming.

    3 credits

    4 credits

  • Mechanics 1

    4 credits

  • Computer Science 1

  • Numerical Methods

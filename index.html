Notes

This is my collection of work in progress lecture notes, which are not direct transcriptions of the lectures but rather draw from various sources (including textbooks and fellow classmates' notes), highly modified to aid my own understanding of the material.

The beautiful $\LaTeX$ template used is entirely based on Adam Kelly's style file.

If you find any errors please contact me at my email address.

Semester 1

  • Real Analysis

    Construction of the reals, supremum and infimum, properties of the reals, point-set topology, Heine-Borel theorem, convergence of sequences and series, Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem, tests for convergence of series.

    4 credits

  • Geometry and Linear Algebra

    Coordinate geometry in 3 dimensions, conicoids, quadric surfaces. Vector spaces, subspaces, linear dependence and independece, basis and span, maximal linearly independent subsets, linear transformations, rank-nullity theorem, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, Cayley-Hamilton theorem.

    4 credits

  • Discrete Mathematics

    Posets, lattice theory, boolean algebra, pigeonhole principle, finite combinatorics, generating functions, graphs and trees, complete graphs, bipartite graphs, connected graphs, Eulerian graphs, Hamiltonian graphs, planar graphs.

    4 credits

  • Probability and Descriptive Statistics

    Random variables, univariate and multivariate probability distributions and densities, expectation, skewness, kurtosis, covariance, linear regression, correlation, Spearman's rank correlation.

    4 credits

  • MATLAB Programming

    An introduction to MATLAB programming.

    3 credits

Semester 2

  • Algebra

    Classical theory of equations, inequalities, elementary number theory, permutations, groups, homomorphism and isomorphism, dihedral group $\textbf{D}_n,$ cosets, orbit, centraliser, normaliser and stabiliser, normal subgroups, finite groups, cyclic groups and Lagrange's theorem, isomorphism theorems, symmetric group $\textbf{S}_n,$ and Cayley's theorem, rings, integral domains and fields.

    4 credits

  • Theory of Real Functions

    Limits and continuity of functions, types of discontinuities, differentiability of functions, Fréchet derivative, Carathéodory's theorem, mean value theorems, approximation of polynomials, Taylor's theorem and power series, and advanced calculus in one real variable.

    4 credits

  • Computer Science 1

    Programming in C and C++.

    4 credits

  • Numerical Methods

    Error analysis, root finding, interpolation of polynomials, numerical integration and differentiation, numerical solutions of differential equations, practical computations in MATLAB.

    3 credits

  • Measure and Integration

    After looking at measurable functions and some types of convergences for such functions, we will introduce the notion of the Lebesgue integral in an abstract measure space and study its properties. We will relate the two important processes of the calculus: differentiation and integration, in the context of the Lebesgue integral. We will also study measures and integrals on product spaces and also signed measures. Finally we will study the important properties of $L^p$ spaces.

    NPTEL course

  • Category Theory

    Categories, hom-sets, duality, isomorphisms and groupoids, functors, Yoneda's lemma, Yoneda embedding, Galois connections, adjoint functor theorem, special adjoint functor theorem, Gabriel-Ulmer duality, monads, Eilenberg-Moore and Kleisli categories, Beck's monadicity theorem, monoidal categories, Grothendieck categories, Gabriel-Zisman multiplicative systems, Serre subcategories, Gabriel-Popescu theorem, Freyd-Mitchell embedding theorem, homological algebra and homotopy theory, Quillen model structure on topological spaces, Quillen adjunction and homotopy categories, topos theory, Heyting algebras, sheaves, Grothendieck topology, Giraud's theorem.

    NPTEL course

  • Computational Commutative Algebra

    Rings and ideals, ring homomorphisms, Hilbert's basis theorem, Hilbert's Nullstellensatz, introduction to Macaulay2, Gröbner bases, ideal membership, solving systems of polynomial rings, modules, associated primes and primary decomposition, integral extensions, integral closure, Noether normalisation, Hilbert functions, dimension theory, applications to geometry, homological algebra: depth, Koszul complex, free resolutions, Auslander-Buchsbaum formula.

    NPTEL course

  • Computational Arithmetic Geometry

    Algebraic curves and function fields, the Riemann-Roch theorem, zeta function of curves over finite fields, the relevant Riemann hypothesis and its proof, $L$-functions, exponential sums, counting points on curves (methods: torsion-based or $p$-adic), integer factoring via curves, algebraic geometric codes, hyperelliptic curve cryptography, computing roots of unity in finite fields.

    NPTEL course

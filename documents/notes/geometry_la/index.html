2.1 Vector Spaces and Subspaces
Deﬁnition 2.1 (Vector Space)
An F-vector space, linear space or vector space over F is an abelian group
of vectors (V, +) with a binary operation (scalar multiplication) deﬁned from an
underlying ﬁeld of scalars F to V as F × V → V : (λ, v) 7→ λv s.t. the foll. axioms
hold:
1. Vector addition: u, v ∈ V =⇒ u + v ∈ V .
a) Commutativity: u + v = v + u ∀u, v ∈ V .
b) Associativity: u + (v + w) = (u + v) + w ∀u, v, w ∈ V .
c) Zero vector: ∃0 ∈ V (called the origin) s.t. v = v + 0 = 0 + v ∀v ∈ V .
d) Additive Inverse: ∀v ∈ V ∃(−v) ∈ V : v + (−v) = (−v) + v = 0.
2. Scalar multiplication: λ ∈ F, v ∈ V =⇒ λv ∈ V .
a) Multiplicative Identity: v = v · 1 = 1 · v ∀v ∈ V .
b) Associativity: (λµ)v = λ(µv) ∀v ∈ V .
3. Distributivity:
a) Distributivity in V : λ(u + v) = λu + λv ∀λ ∈ F, u, v ∈ V .
b) Distributivity in F: (λ + µ)v = λv + µv ∀λ, µ ∈ F, v ∈ V .

Theorem 2.3 (Cancellation Law)
Let V be an F-vector space and x, y, z ∈ V s.t. x + z = y + z, then x = y.
Proof. From the vector space axioms, ∃(−z) ∈ V s.t. z + (−z) = 0. (additive
inverse)
Thus,
x, y, z ∈ V : x + z = y + z =⇒ (x + z) + (−z) = (y + z) + (−z)
=⇒ x + (z + (−z)) = y + (z + (−z)) =⇒ x + 0 = y + 0 =⇒ x = y.

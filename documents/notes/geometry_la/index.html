Geometry

Poles and polars

The polar of a point w.r.t. a conic is the locus of the point of intersection of the tangents to the conic at the extremities of the chords drawn through that point. More precisely, if the chords of a conic be drawn through a fixed point and tangents be drawn at the extremities of all such chords, then the locus of the point of intersection of these tangents to the conic is called the polar of the fixed point which itself is called the pole.
The terms pole and polar are thus correlative.

Equation of the polar w.r.t. a conic

Let the equation of the conic be \[\displaystyle ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0\ ... (1) \] Let \(AB\) be a chord passing through the point \(P(x_1,\ y_1)\) and let the tangets at \(A\) and \(B\) meet at the point \(T(x_2,\ y_2)\). The locus of the point \(T\) is the polar, \(P\) being the pole. Now \(AB\) is the chord of contact of the tangents drawn from \(T(x_2,\ y_2)\) to the conic. So its equation \[\displaystyle axx_2 + h(xy_2 + yx_2) + byy_2 + g(x+x_2) + f(y+y_2) + c = 0 \] passes through the point \(P(x_1,\ y_1)\). Thus \[\displaystyle ax_1x_2 + h(x_1y_2 + y_1x_2) + by_1y_2 + g(x_1+x_2) + f(y_1+y_2) + c = 0. \] It shows that the locus of \((x_2,\ y_2)\) is the line \[\displaystyle axx_1 + h(xy_1 + yx_1) + byy_1 + g(x+x_1) + f(y+y_1) + c = 0\ ... (2) \] Thus, \((2)\) is the polar of \((x_1,\ y_1)\) w.r.t. the conic \((1)\).

Find the equation of the polar of a point \((x_1,\ y_1)\) w.r.t. the circle \(x^2 + y^2 = a^2\).
Toggle solution

The equation of the circle is \[\displaystyle x^2 + y^2 = a^2 \ ... (1) \] Let \(AB\) be a chord passing through the point \(P(x_1,\ y_1)\) and let the tangets at \(A\) and \(B\) meet at the point \(T(x_2,\ y_2)\). The locus of \(T\) will be the polar of our pole \(P\) w.r.t. \((1)\).
Now \(AB\) is the chord of contact of the tangents drawn from \(T\) to the \((1)\). So its equation is \(xx_2 + yy_2 = a^2\).
This passes through the pole \(P\). \[ \therefore\ x_1x_2 + y_1y_2 = a^2 \] and hence the locus of \(T\) is \(xx_1 + yy_1 = a^2\), which is the required equation of the polar of \(P\) w.r.t. \((1)\).
